## ✅ What Earns It the B

### 1. Vehicle and Equipment Variety — Exceptional
With over 250 vehicles and equipment from more than 75 manufacturers — including real-world brands like Challenger, Fendt, Valtra, and Massey Ferguson — the breadth of licensed machinery on offer is genuinely impressive and a massive draw for the target audience.

### 2. Deep Farming Systems That Actually Simulate Farming
The game is far more complex than it appears on the surface. There are 10 different crops across 23 different fields, a three-stage growing process, three varieties of fertiliser, and a supply-and-demand economy where each selling point offers different prices — actively pushing you to diversify your crops and manage your farm strategically. For a simulator, this is exactly what you want.

### 3. Mod Support — A Game Changer
FS17 was the first entry in the series to bring mod support to consoles, not just PC. The modding community dramatically extends the game's lifespan well beyond its base content, adding vehicles, maps, equipment, and entirely new gameplay possibilities over time.

### 4. Surprisingly Huge Playtime
The main story takes around 55 hours, side content brings that to over 106 hours, and a completionist run can reach 154 hours. For a single purchase, that is extraordinary value if the genre clicks with you — few games at any price point offer this kind of longevity.

### 5. The Zen Factor Is Real
There is something strangely soothing and satisfying about the experience — country tunes wafting from your tractor's radio, the soft droning of the harvester as you collect crops, the slow rhythm of building a farm from debt into a thriving operation. It is an acquired taste, but once it clicks, it really clicks.

---

## ❌ Why It Doesn't Go Higher

### 1. Brutal for Newcomers — Almost No Hand-Holding
Tutorials cover only the bare basics, and much of the game depends on trial-and-error from the very start. Dropping players into debt from day one with minimal guidance is a design choice that will immediately alienate a large portion of players who don't already have patience for the genre.

### 2. Deeply Niche — Fails Outside Its Audience
Players with no appetite for slow-paced, repetitive gameplay will find very little to hold onto here. The game makes almost no effort to broaden its appeal, and that unapologetic narrowness keeps it from being something that can be widely recommended.

### 3. Visuals Are Just Adequate
The machinery and landscapes are serviceable, but the cosmetic component doesn't match the depth of the game's systems. Character models, village environments, and interiors feel underdeveloped for a 2016 release, and a lot of the scenery exists purely for show.

### 4. Personality Is Nearly Absent
It is a simulator in the truest, driest sense of the word. Beyond the zen of repetitive farming loops, there is almost no charm, humor, or narrative personality injected into the experience to keep players who need more than mechanics engaged.

## 🏁 Final Verdict

Farming Simulator 17 is a game that knows exactly what it is and makes absolutely no apologies for it. If you love farming, management systems, and the meditative grind of building something from nothing, this is **A tier material**. If you are a general gamer looking for excitement, narrative, or polish, it will feel much closer to **E tier**. The **B tier** placement is the honest middle ground — a genuinely deep and rewarding simulator held back by zero accessibility, thin visuals, and an almost aggressive indifference to anyone outside its core fanbase.

> **Who should play it:** Farming enthusiasts, management sim lovers, players who want hundreds of hours of relaxed sandbox gameplay, and anyone who enjoys deep modding communities.
>
> **Who should skip it:** Anyone expecting action, narrative, visual polish, or a welcoming onboarding experience.
