## Tier: **C**

> *A game with a genuinely unique setting and emotional concept — let down by shallow gameplay, weak voice acting, and the fatal flaw of being a horror game that isn't scary.*

## ✅ What Saves It From Going Lower

### 1. Atmosphere Is Its One Genuine Strength
Through the Woods nails creepy atmosphere and has an interesting story that draws on Norse mythology — something that gives it a uniquely distinct feeling in the horror genre. The Norwegian forest setting is unlike anything else in horror gaming, and when the game leans into it, it works.

### 2. Smart Environmental Tension
The game uses ambient sound and environment design cleverly. Disturbing noises hint at creatures lurking in the shadows — sometimes it's just a scare tactic, and sometimes there really is something there waiting for you. This uncertainty is the game at its best.

### 3. Emotionally Mature Story Concept
The core narrative deals with a strained mother-son relationship, guilt, grief, and the weight of neglect — heavy themes for a horror game to tackle. The writers don't shy away from uncomfortable subjects surrounding the main character's backstory, and it delivers some genuinely dramatic moments. The ending in particular is a highlight.

---

## ❌ Why It Lands at C and Not Higher

### 1. It's Not Actually Scary — Its Core Job
This is the most damning flaw. Through the Woods doesn't feel scary. Not even once. When a horror game fails at being horrifying, everything else has to fight an enormous uphill battle to compensate — and this game can't quite manage it.

### 2. Gameplay Is Nearly Non-Existent
The entire mechanical loop across its 3-hour runtime is: walk, find notes, run away from things, shine a torch. The game relies too heavily on atmosphere and never does much to evolve beyond it. There is no combat, no puzzle system worth noting, no meaningful player agency.

### 3. Voice Acting Is a Serious Weak Point
All of the recorded dialogue sounds stiff and unnatural. For a narrative-driven game where the mother Karen's voiceover carries the emotional weight of the entire story, this is a significant problem. The performances undercut moments that should land hard.

### 4. Painfully Short With No Replay Value
The game clocks in at roughly 3 to 4 hours with no meaningful reason to return after the credits roll. Even walking simulators need to justify their length — Through the Woods barely does.

### 5. Visuals Are a Mixed Bag
The landscapes, foliage, and skies are at times genuinely beautiful and atmospheric. But character models are rough around the edges, villages feel generic, and the overall visual palette feels flat and underpowered for what the game is trying to evoke.

## 🏁 Final Verdict

Through the Woods is a game of genuine ambition trapped inside severe execution limitations. The Norse mythology backdrop is inspired, the emotional core of the story is solid, and the environmental atmosphere has real moments of brilliance. But a horror game that isn't scary, paired with almost no real gameplay and weak voice performances, cannot climb higher than **C Tier** — no matter how interesting its bones are.

> **Who should play it:** Norse mythology enthusiasts, players who love atmospheric walking sims, and those who want a short, moody story experience on sale.
>
> **Who should skip it:** Anyone expecting genuine horror, deep gameplay mechanics, or a full-length experience.
